# Research Summary

## Merlion Park

**History & Cultural Significance**
The Merlion, Singapore's official mascot, combines a lion's head with a fish's body, symbolising the island's origins as a fishing village (Temasek) and the legendary sighting of a lion by Sang Nila Utama, which led to the name *Singapura* (Lion City). Conceived in 1964 by Fraser Brunner, the first statue was unveiled on 15 September 1972 at the mouth of the Singapore River. It stands 8.6 m tall, weighing 15 tonnes, and has become an iconic icon featured on tourism posters, currency, and stamps, representing Singapore’s transformation into a modern metropolis.

**Visitor Information (2024)**
- **Opening Hours:** Open 24 hours a day, every day of the year.
- **Admission:** Free of charge.
- **Location:** 1 Fullerton Rd, Merlion Park, Singapore 049213.

## Marina Bay Sands

**Architectural Features**
Marina Bay Sands (MBS) is an integrated resort opened on 27 April 2010. Designed by architect Moshe Safdie and engineered by Arup, the complex comprises three 55‑storey hotel towers linked by the iconic SkyPark – a 340 m long cantilevered platform that overhangs the north tower by 67 m. Highlights include the world’s largest rooftop infinity pool, the lotus‑inspired ArtScience Museum, a 2,561‑room hotel, a 15,000‑seat arena, a casino, and The Shoppes mall. Construction cost S$8 billion (US$6.5 billion).

**Visitor Information (2024 – SkyPark Observation Deck)**

- **Location:** 10 Bayfront Ave, Singapore 018956.
